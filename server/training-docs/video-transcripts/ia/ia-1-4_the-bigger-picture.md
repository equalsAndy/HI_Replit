# The Bigger Picture

**Step ID:** ia-1-4
**Workshop:** ia

---

In this learning unit, we widen the lens to see imagination as a deep human force, one that's shaped civilizations, guided morality, and continues to define our future. Long before electricity, imagination was known for what it is. Abhinavagupta saw it not as fantasy, but as the force that shapes reality.

Imagination can create or destroy reality. Moral imagination ensures our visions are guided by fairness, compassion, and responsibility. Ozick captures imagination's highest function. Its ability to envision what lies beyond the limits of the known and the familiar. Visionaries, artists, and entire cultures have pictured and embraced the power that Abhinavagupta and Ozick describe.

Imagination is not abstract. It's an active layer of human experience woven into how societies make meaning, generate possibility, and shape reality. Many traditions — Vedic, Tibetan, Vodou, shamanic — treat imagination and reality as interwoven, affirming intuition, mystery, and symbolic insight as genuine sources of knowledge.

Humanity has also learned caution. Imagination can drift into fantasy, hallucination, and delusion. The line between vision and insanity is often thin. Black swan events reveal that life holds realities that exist beyond our knowledge, but not our imagination. History's darkest moments came not just from a lack of imagination, but from its abuse, misuse, or total absence.

When imagination fails us, so does our humanity. This is a pivotal moment in human history unlike any recorded. AI now exploits both our self-awareness gap and imagination deficit. We can reclaim our agency or become passive consumers of someone else's dreams. The neuroscience is clear. Self-awareness and imagination can be strengthened through deliberate practice, reshaping the brain and enhancing the mind at all ages through training.

These enduring capabilities — imagination, curiosity, caring, creativity, and courage — underpin the core skills needed for 2030 and beyond. They give you a stable inner foundation in a world where technical skills quickly expire. Artificial intelligence forces humanity to revalue our natural intelligence. When we reclaim our agency, a new dynamic equilibrium will emerge.

We designed this DMN icon to give the human inner engine, our own GPU, a beautiful visible identity, a reminder of the power AI cannot replace, but rather depends upon. These paired icons represent the dynamic balance we must now manage. Honoring human agency while using AI wisely and intentionally. Simple, engaging activities like flying a kite activate the DMN, naturally strengthening imagination, reflection, and inner coherence.

Da Vinci wrote in his journal, "It should not be hard for you to stop sometimes and look into the stains of walls or ashes of a fire or clouds. You may find really marvelous ideas." In module two, you'll learn how imagination amplifies core human capabilities and why enhancing these is essential in the AI era.
