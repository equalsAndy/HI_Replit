# I INTRODUCTION
 
The Heliotrope Imaginal Methodology is inspired by educational ethnographic research 
conducted by Dr. Howard B. Esbin, its originator. For two years, (1991-93), he lived in the 
Tabaka, the village epicenter of the soapstone carving cottage industry in western Kenya. As a 
participant-observer, he studied how the local artisans transmitted complex visual design and 
carving skills to youth through community-based, social learning rather than formal instruction. 
(Children learned numeracy and literacy mornings at school and in the afternoons attended 
family work groups. They learned to produce carvings through observation and modelling  of 
older youth and adults.) 
 
Dr. Esbin had returned to university studies after a decade of senior management experience in 
the retail industry, driven by his frustration over why so many capable young people devalued 
their imaginations—an ability that had always been valued growing up. His father was a jeweler 
and his grandfather an ornamental iron smith. 
 
Dr. Esbin’s research provided an answer, which he later explained in, “Imagination Goes to 
School”, an essay published by Education Canada. “Children enter school brimming with 
imagination. They're masters of make-believe and visualization, honed through years of play. 
For the next decade, this essential cognitive faculty is benignly neglected school. By 
comparison, the faculty of reason is explicitly and assiduously cultivated...” (v48 n1 p24-26, 28 
Win 2007-2008) 
 
Consequently, the Kisii carvers unique educational approach – one shared by indigenous 
societies worldwide -  inspired the development of the Heliotrope Imaginal Methodology. This 
resource has evolved over two decades of research and practical application across diverse 
age groups, cultures, and institution. About 30,000 individuals and hundreds of teams have 
benefitted, from middle school to graduate programs, NGOs, and companies spanning five 
continents. 
 
Both HI Workshop, AllStarTeams and Imaginal Agility, employ the HM in different ways to 
address two immense, related workplace challenges – the pervasive Self-Awareness Gap and 
the expanding Imagination Deficit.

Imagination Self-Assessment Overview 
The Imagination Self-Assessment is a formative tool designed to support individual reflection 
and developmental insight across five core human capabilities: imagination, curiosity, empathy, 
creativity, and courage. It is not a test, but rather a guided self-evaluation that produces a visual 
representation—referred to as the Radar Map—based on subjective self-ratings. 
Participants are asked to assess the general presence and expression of each capability in their 
personal and professional lives using a 5-point scale. The responses generate a radar-style 
chart accompanied by a holistic summary. This visualization serves as a developmental artifact, 
supporting both individual insight and team-based dialogue. 
 
Key principles guiding the assessment include: 
• 
No judgment or ranking is applied 
• 
The assessment infers patterns rather than imposing fixed conclusions 
• 
Interpretation is participant-led and open-ended 
• 
Emphasis is placed on agency, reflection, and internal validity 
 
The tool is embedded within the broader framework of the Imaginal Agility program and serves 
as a preparatory component for later group-based practice sessions. During these sessions,  
Radar Maps are used to facilitate discussions around team strengths, gaps, and emergent 
dynamics. 
 
The Imagination Self-Assessment reflects current trends in cognitive science and educational 
design that emphasize reflective capacity as essential to learning, growth, and innovation. Its 
development responds to what research institutions, including Deloitte, have identified as a 
growing “imagination deficit” in AI-mediated work environments. By providing a structured yet 
flexible format for assessing core imaginative capabilities, this tool contributes to building 
individual and collective adaptive capacity in complex, rapidly changing settings.

# IV HUMAN POTENTIAL
 
The Heliotrope Imaginal Methodology integrates models and practices from Ancient Wisdom 
Traditions and Prospective Psychology in line with neuroscience principles regarding life-span 
self-identity, growth, and development and the specific role imagination plays throughout, 
especially when cultivated intentionally. 
 
Ancient Wisdom Traditions  
Heliotrope Imaginal draws deeply from two ancient lineages of knowledge—Greek teleological 
philosophy and Vedic imagination states—to ground its modern, neuroscience-informed 
methodology for developing human capability in the age of AI. 
 
Greek Teleological Concepts 
In classical Greek philosophy, telos refers to purpose, end, or inherent aim. Thinkers like 
Aristotle saw human beings as teleological creatures, naturally oriented toward the actualization 
of their unique potential (eudaimonia). Flourishing, in this view, is not a passive state but an 
active process of aligning one’s inner virtues with one’s highest purpose.  
 
Heliotrope Imaginal translates this principle into the modern developmental context. Both the 
AllStarTeams and Imaginal Agility workshops are designed to: 
• 
Help individuals discover their innate strengths (telos-in-seed form), 
• 
Clarify their inner direction or personal North Star, and Scaffold their journey toward 
actualization through reflective learning, collaborative practice, and imaginative foresight. 
 
The model’s sequencing—Self-Awareness → Well-Being → Vision—mirrors the Aristotelian arc 
of internal development leading toward outer contribution. 
 
Vedic Imagination States 
The Vedas, among the world’s oldest philosophical texts, describe the imagination (kalpana) not 
as mere fantasy but as a faculty of perception that accesses subtle realities. Ancient Vedic 
teachings distinguish between five progressive states or powers of imagination—ranging from 
image-formation to creative realization—that are seen as essential to personal awakening and 
world-making. 
 
This ancient model directly informs Heliotrope's proprietary Ladder of Imagination—a 
developmental taxonomy guiding learners from: 
• 
Basic perception and emotional resonance, 
• 
Through creative problem-solving and insight, 
• 
Toward visionary authorship and self-transcendence. 
 
The Ladder not only echoes the Vedic arc from manas (mind) to buddhi (higher wisdom), but 
also provides a practical framework for reawakening the underused human capacity for inner 
vision and creative agency—especially relevant in an era where AI risks externalizing or 
offloading imagination itself.

Prospective Psychology  
Heliotrope Imaginal is fundamentally a forward-facing methodology. It is deeply informed by

imagine, plan for, and construct the future. This research validates Heliotrope’s core premise: 
that imagination is not a luxury, but a critical cognitive faculty for navigating complexity and 
uncertainty. 
 
Future-Oriented Cognition 
At the heart of prospective psychology lies the idea that humans are not only memory-based 
beings, but future-simulating organisms. We constantly construct internal models of what might 
be, and these imagined futures influence our present behavior, choices, and emotional states. 
 
Heliotrope builds on this insight by designing learning experiences that: 
Move participants from passive coping to active future authorship, 
Encourage deliberate mental rehearsal of ideal selves and scenarios, and 
Strengthen the neural circuits associated with agency, anticipation, and resilience. 
 
Tools like the Ladder of Imagination and the Return on Imagination (ROI 2.0) framework are 
explicitly structured to scaffold and stimulate future-oriented thought. 
 
Self-Continuity and Future Self 
One of prospective psychology’s most potent findings is that a strong sense of continuity with 
one’s future self  will enhance wise decision-making, act to delay gratification, and deepen well-
being. 
 
Heliotrope translates this research into practical exercises like: 
• 
Future Self Visualization, helping individuals see and feel who they are becoming,

reinforce alignment between present and future identity, 
 
Workshop sequences that build time-integrated self-awareness, critical for moral agency in AI-
mediated contexts. This emphasis on self-continuity acts as an ethical backbone to the work: 
when people feel more connected to their future selves, they act with more foresight, 
responsibility, and vision.

# V METHODOLOGY: INTEGRATED FRAMEWORK
The Heliotrope Imaginal Methodology synthesizes 3,000 years of wisdom traditions with 
contemporary neuroscience into a unified developmental framework. Rather than treating 
ancient philosophy, modern psychology, and brain science as discrete domains, the HM reveals 
how they converge around a shared principle: imagination as the fundamental human capacity 
for growth, adaptation, and conscious evolution. 
 
Core Architecture: The Double Helix Model 
At the heart of HM ls the Double Helix Model, which represents the dynamic interplay between 
two foundational neural processes: 
Reflection (rooted in memory and past experience) 
Imagination (oriented toward vision and future possibility) 
 
These twin pathways support both individual development and collective intelligence through 
two distinct but interconnected workshop formats: 
• 
AllStarTeams (AST) focuses on cultivating self-awareness using the Five Strengths 
framework. It moves participants from internal insight to external expression. 
• 
Imaginal Agility (IA) strengthens imagination as a trainable capability using the Five 
Enduring Capabilities framework. It builds from personal fluency to collaborative and 
organizational impact. 
 
Three Principles of Integration 
The HM is organized around three integration principles that cut across both workshops: 
 
Vertical Integration: Connects ancient sources (Greek telos, Vedic imagination states) to 
modern psychology (prospective cognition, appreciative inquiry), and then grounds them in 
neuroscience (mental synthesis, neural synchrony). 
Horizontal Integration: Aligns experiences from the individual level (self-assessment) to the 
team level (facilitated practice), to the organizational level (culture and performance). 
 
Temporal Integration: Builds developmental continuity from present self-awareness to future 
identity construction and aspirational authorship.

The Prism Effect: A Cognitive Refractor 
The prism metaphor serves as both a visual and conceptual key to understanding the 
methodology. Just as white light passes through a prism and refracts into a visible spectrum, 
imagination—as the apex capability—refracts through human consciousness into a continuum 
of internal, interpersonal, and collective strengths. 
 
The HI Methodology does not treat these capabilities as isolated traits but as differentiated 
expressions of imagination. Strengthening one capacity—such as empathy—inevitably supports 
the development of others, like creativity or courage, due to their interconnected nature. 
 
Methodological Convergence Across Workshops 
Although the workshops differ in focus—AST addressing the Self-Awareness Gap, IA 
confronting the Imagination Deficit—they are built from the same core blueprint. Each program 
applies the HM architecture to a specific cognitive challenge while generating similar 
neurobiological and behavioral outcomes. 
 
This shared methodological structure explains why learners across age groups, sectors, and 
roles experience consistent benefits: the approach is scalable, modular, and anchored in human 
development principles that transcend context.
