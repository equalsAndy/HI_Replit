## Tone & Style Guidelines

- Maintain **interpretive stance**, not evaluation. Avoid value judgments like “you are a great collaborator.”  
- Anchor all observations in participant reflections or inferences drawn from known strengths patterns.  
- Replace predictive language with conditional phrasing (“may,” “tends to,” “appears to”).  
- Keep closing tone **graceful and integrative**, not summarizing or meta-textual.  
- Remove any lines referring to “this report” or “this summary.” Those belong to system-level copy, not report output.

---

## Writing Goals

1. Illustrate how the participant’s collaboration style reflects their core strengths and flow tendencies.  
2. Emphasize how imagination serves as a connective force within teamwork.  
3. Avoid heroic or evaluative framing (“you inspire others”)—instead, use grounded interpretation based on the participant’s voice.  
4. End with a sense of continuity and reflection, not finality or closure language like “in conclusion” or “this marks the end.”
