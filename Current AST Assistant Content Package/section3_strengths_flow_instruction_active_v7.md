## Tone and Perspective Guidelines

- Write in **second person** throughout (“you”).  
- Use participant reflections and context-based inference—never predictions.  
- Avoid absolute phrasing (“you always,” “you naturally,” “you tend to”) unless directly quoted.  
- Favor interpretive framing: “Flow may flourish when…,” “Your reflections suggest…,” “This interplay can create…,” etc.  
- Imagination should appear as a subtle integrator rather than a personified “conductor.”  
- Maintain consistency with the language of Sections 1 and 2 for seamless narrative continuity.

---

## Writing Goals

1. Describe how the participant’s leading strengths combine to support or challenge flow.  
2. Identify how imagination bridges or restores balance among strengths when friction occurs.  
3. Integrate examples or phrases drawn directly from participant reflections.  
4. Retain a contemplative rhythm consistent with the rest of the report.

## Narrative Model Example (for tone and structure)

Integration unfolds as the meeting place of your strengths and flow, where energy, thought, and feeling converge into rhythm. Each strength contributes its own movement—sometimes in harmony, sometimes in contrast—creating a living pattern that evolves as you shift between contexts.

[[visual:combo1]]
Flow may flourish when these strengths interact dynamically, aligning decisiveness with emotional intelligence and turning collaboration into momentum. Acting and Feeling often appear as co-leads, translating intention into action while maintaining attunement to others. Planning provides the structure that steadies this energy, and Thinking refines it, offering focus where intuition or urgency might otherwise dominate.

Your reflections suggest attentiveness to both emotional and structural dynamics. You’ve described moments when shared focus helps the team “move toward the goal with purpose,” and times when lack of clarity can “slow the rhythm.” These observations show how your internal strengths architecture both adapts to and shapes your collaborative environments.

Within these patterns, imagination may function as the integrator—a quiet but constant sense-making presence that reconnects insight with action. Rather than acting as a separate “conductor,” imagination weaves between strengths, helping you notice when one energy—such as drive, empathy, or analysis—overextends. Through imagination, you find the rhythm again, not by forcing balance but by perceiving it differently.

When friction arises, it becomes a moment for recalibration rather than correction. A missed connection, an unclear goal, or a pause in momentum does not break the system—it reveals where imagination can re-enter. You’ve shared reflections about regaining focus after disorganization or tension, pointing to how awareness itself becomes the first step toward realignment.

Imagination therefore serves as the quiet integrator across your system of strengths and flow—less a conductor and more a resonant thread, allowing balance, adaptability, and renewal to re-emerge when complexity increases. This view honors both the distinct shape of your strengths and the subtle fluidity that allows them to operate together.
