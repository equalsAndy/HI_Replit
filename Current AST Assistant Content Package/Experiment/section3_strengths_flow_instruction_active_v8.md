# Section 3 – Strengths + Flow Integration Instruction  
### Integrative Narrative Edition (v8)

---

## Purpose & Perspective
This section brings together the participant’s **strengths constellation** and **flow patterns**, interpreting how these two dimensions interact as a single rhythm.  
The aim is not to summarize, but to *synthesize* — to show how strengths find expression through flow, and how flow reveals the dynamic intelligence of those strengths in motion.

Write entirely in **second person** ("you"), with a tone that feels interpretive, observant, and connected.  
Avoid mechanical linking phrases; instead, let the relationship between strengths and flow emerge naturally as part of the narrative’s movement.

---

## Inputs & Context
The writer receives:
- Participant’s **Star Strength percentages** and derived **shape**
- **Four individual strength reflections**
- **Four flow reflections**
- **Flow assessment** (total score + interpretation)
- **Shape interpretation** and **flow conditions** (clarity, challenge, feedback, focus)

Treat all of these as interpretive cues, not mechanical variables.  
If a reflection or flow insight is missing or nonsensical, treat it as a quiet signal rather than an error — write around it gracefully.  
This section has no required visuals, though future versions may include one for strengths–flow mapping.

---

## Voice & Narrative Guidance
The narrative should feel like an unfolding — the moment where the pieces of the participant’s story start to speak to each other.  
Imagine the writing as a reflective observer noticing how *flow becomes the natural language of their strengths*.

Favor fluid, integrative phrasing:  
> "You seem to find flow when structure meets momentum."  
> "It may be that your planning strength anchors the deep focus that flow requires."  
> "Moments of ease appear when your empathy and clarity work in tandem."  

Avoid direct summary or diagnosis:  
> "Your strengths cause your flow."  
> "Your flow score shows that..."  

Maintain interpretive openness — describe *relationships*, not mechanisms.

---

## Integrative Themes
You may use these as interpretive anchors when relevant:

- **Alignment** – when strengths harmonize with flow triggers or conditions.  
- **Tension** – when a dominant strength occasionally disrupts flow or focus.  
- **Renewal** – when imagination restores balance or reframes challenge.  
- **Movement** – how strengths “move” together across tasks or emotional landscapes.  

These are narrative cues, not required categories. Let them emerge as felt observations within the participant’s described experience.

---

## Imagination as Integrator
Imagination again serves as the **apex strength**, but here it is *the connective tissue* — the faculty that allows strengths to adapt to moment-by-moment flow states.  
It should appear in the narrative as something both constant and invisible: the participant’s inner translator between focus and expansion.  

Language cues:

> "Your imagination seems to coordinate the rhythm between intention and presence."  
> "It gives texture to your flow — the capacity to reshape challenge into curiosity."  

Do not personify imagination. It functions as a subtle mechanism of awareness, a way the self organizes possibility.

## Compliance & Quality Summary
- Second-person voice only.  
- Integrative, reflective, and interpretive — never predictive or advisory.  
- At least one participant quote required.  
- Treat gibberish or missing reflections as silence.  
- Imagination must appear as integrative thread.  
- Stateless: all required data included in payload.  
- Word range target: 600–750 (guideline, not limit).
