# Section 1 – Strengths & Imagination Instruction  
### Integrative Narrative Edition (v11)

---

## Purpose & Perspective
This section introduces the participant’s **strengths constellation** as a living pattern rather than a static profile. The aim is to interpret—not diagnose—how their Thinking, Feeling, Acting, and Planning energies interrelate, forming a rhythm that expresses how they navigate work, relationships, and creative flow.  

Write entirely in **second person** ("you"), keeping tone reflective, grounded, and imaginative. The narrative should feel like an *interpretation of a living system*, not a list of traits.

---

## Inputs & Context
The writer receives:
- Participant’s **Star Strength percentages** (Thinking, Feeling, Acting, Planning)
- **Shape data** derived from those percentages (Balanced, One High, Two High + Two Low, etc.)
- **Strength reflections** (1–4) and the **Imagination reflection**
- Team Values & Unique Contribution reflections for contextual tone

Use these as interpretive material, not mechanical references. If any reflection is missing or nonsensical (e.g., gibberish), treat it as silence rather than data—write around it gracefully.

---

## Voice & Narrative Guidance
Your voice should read as *attentive interpretation* rather than analysis.  Imagine a thoughtful narrator helping the reader notice what their data is already saying.  Use rhythm and flow between sentences—let imagery like balance, constellation, rhythm, or mirror appear naturally but sparingly.  

Favor **speculative phrasing**:
> "You describe moments when..."  
> "It may be that your strength in planning lends calm where momentum builds."  
> "This balance suggests..."  

Avoid categorical phrasing:
> "You always..."  
> "You will..."  
> "Your profile shows..."  

Integrate participant quotes verbatim when possible. A single reflection quote per section is ideal.

---

Visuals should feel like part of the story—introduce them gently instead of as commands. Example phrasing:

> "Your StarCard chart below captures this constellation visually—a snapshot before we translate numbers into story."

Then include the RML declaration at the top of the section, followed by inline placeholders before relevant descriptions.

---

## Shape & Strength Interplay
Interpret **shape** as the rhythm through which strengths relate, not a label that confines them.

**Balanced** – all four near equal; energy shifts fluidly.  
**One High** – a single domain leads strongly; others offer support.  
**Two High + Two Low** – pairs in conversation, creating contrast and dual focus.  
**Three High + One Low** – one quiet domain that defines balance through absence.  
**Stair-Step / Gradient** – natural sequencing; attention flows from one to the next.  

If differences fall within ~2 percent, note fluidity instead of contrast. Use comparison language gently:
> "Closer to Balanced than Stair-Step, your distribution hints at versatility more than contrast."

---

## Imagination Integration
Imagination should appear as the **apex strength**—not an addition, but the connective current that harmonizes all others. It bridges insight (Thinking), empathy (Feeling), structure (Planning), and momentum (Acting). 

Language cues:
> "Imagination weaves between your domains, revealing harmony and contrast alike."  
> "It serves as the quiet integrator—the thread through which each strength finds expression."  

Do not personify imagination or treat it as external guidance. It is a human capacity always present, subtly organizing awareness and creativity.

## Compliance & Quality Summary
- Second-person perspective only.  
- Interpretive, reflective, speculative; never predictive or advisory.  
- Include one participant quote minimum.  
- Treat gibberish or missing reflections as silence.  
- Include RML declarations and placeholders correctly.  
- Use imagination as integrative apex strength.  
- Word target for generated section: 600–750 (guideline only).
